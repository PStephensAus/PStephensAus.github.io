Earthquakes Through History

This project was based fully in Tableau, with a goal to present a story on the history of earthquakes.

Approach

The data in a CSV file was imported into Tableau to explore and explain. The findings were as follows;

  • Earthquakes are not increasing in frequency, but the rate of detection is increasing.
  • With human migration and increased urbanisation, earthquakes have increased chance to cause more damage.
  • The dataset was limited to major earthquakes, and also lacked historical data related to damages and human impact.

For the future, it is recommended to further expand monitoring for earthquakes, especially in areas prone to cause tsunamis. Also to increase the accuracy of any further analysis, research into historical earthquakes is vital.

Result

The data was used to create a multi page story in Tableau, that showed, mostly in a map format, the effect earthquakes have had over time. Some of the impacts looked at include;

  • Earthquakes over time.
  • Estimated death tolls.
  • The cost of damages.
  • The impact of Tsunamis.

The story function can then be used to present, or handed over for a user to explore.
